# Mentora Demo Day Pitch Deck Guide

This document describes the English pitch deck structure for Mentora. It is not a speaking script. Use it as a slide-by-slide content guide for rewriting, generating, or reviewing the final Demo Day deck.

## Deck Principle

Mentora should be positioned as a **student-first, course-grounded AI tutor for AI learners in intensive cohorts**.

Do not over-position the current product as a mature mentor dashboard or Learning Ops platform. The student learning experience is the polished core. Mentor/operator value should appear as buyer context, pilot reporting, and future expansion, not as the main product promise.

The strongest one-liner is:

> Mentora helps AI cohort learners understand, practice, and track progress from their official course materials, not generic AI answers.

The title-slide version can be sharper:

> The course-grounded AI tutor for learners who need to understand, not copy.

Use this positioning logic:

- Target user: AI learners in intensive cohorts.
- Desired outcome: understand, practice, and track progress.
- Differentiated approach: cited Socratic hints + adaptive practice from official course materials.
- Old way: generic AI answers + static practice.

The deck must be sharp enough for Demo Day:

- 9 main slides maximum.
- One main message per slide.
- One dominant visual per slide.
- Maximum 3 short bullets on main slides.
- No paragraph blocks.
- No dense algorithm slides in the main deck.
- Use appendix for technical depth, roadmap, sources, algorithms, and extra validation.

## Visual System

- Use the Mentora/Sapia palette: soft avocado background, strong green, yellow, orange, blue, and dark charcoal.
- Avoid purple, magenta, and generic AI-gradient styling.
- Keep the deck professional, warm, academic, and product-led.
- Use mascots as supporting characters only.
- Sofi/Sofia represents the trusted Socratic tutor.
- Edo represents the learner navigating a difficult AI course.
- Charts, product screenshots, funnels, comparison tables, and simple system diagrams should dominate.
- If a slide has both mascot and chart, the chart wins.

## Recommended Deck Order

1. Title
2. Problem + Learner
3. Solution
4. Product Demo Story
5. Early Usage
6. Market Wedge
7. GTM / Buyer Path
8. Competition / Moat
9. Team + Ask
10+. Appendix

`Learner Persona` is merged into the Problem slide. `Roadmap` moves to appendix. `Why Now` is removed from the main deck. If needed, say one line inside Problem: `AI is already in the classroom.`

## 1. Title

**Goal:** Establish the product category and contrast in one glance.

**Main message:** Mentora helps learners understand, not copy.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Mentora: the course-grounded AI tutor for learners who need to understand, not copy.
```

**Recommended subline:**

```text
Understand, practice, and track progress from official course materials.
```

**Optional spoken one-liner:**

```text
Mentora helps AI cohort learners understand, practice, and track progress from their official course materials, not generic AI answers.
```

**Visual:**

- Mentora logo.
- Clean first-viewport signal.
- Sofi and Edo may appear together, but do not make the slide childish.
- Avoid long mission statements and generic phrases like `AI-powered personalized learning platform`.

## 2. Problem + Learner

**Goal:** Make the learner pain visceral and concrete.

**Main message:** AI learners are using AI to finish work, but they still do not know what they understand.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
AI learners are using AI to finish work — but they still do not know what they understand.
```

**Learner moment:**

```text
Meet Edo: stuck before a lab deadline, surrounded by generic AI answers.
```

**Pain cards:**

- Generic AI gives direct answers.
- Practice is not matched to skill level.
- Learners do not know what to review next.

**Evidence card:**

```text
59,191 reach across 6 AI-learning posts
```

Small label:

```text
Early signal, not PMF.
```

**Important framing:**

Do not describe `1:1 tutoring` as the pain point. That is an alternative solution. The pain is unclear understanding, non-personalized practice, and generic AI answers that can bypass learning.

**Visual:**

- Edo as the concrete learner.
- One large evidence card.
- Three compact pain cards.
- Keep the slide evidence-led, not text-led.

## 3. Solution

**Goal:** Show Mentora’s core loop without technical jargon.

**Main message:** Mentora turns official course materials into hints, practice, and next steps.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Mentora turns official course materials into hints, practice, and next steps.
```

**Outcome bullets:**

- Cited Socratic hints from course materials.
- Adaptive practice matched to current skill.
- Mastery updates that show what to review next.

**Visual:**

Use one learner-first loop:

```text
Course docs -> Cited Socratic hints -> Adaptive practice -> Mastery update -> Next review focus
```

**Avoid on this slide:**

- RAG jargon.
- Elo/BKT/ZPD.
- LinUCB.
- Embedding details.
- Strong mentor dashboard claims.

Those belong in appendix.

## 4. Product Demo Story

**Goal:** Make the product feel real through a learner problem-result story, not a UI walkthrough.

**Main message:** From stuck to next step without giving away the answer.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
From stuck to next step — without giving away the answer.
```

**Demo story:**

1. Edo is stuck on a course concept before a lab.
2. Mentora gives cited Socratic hints from official course material.
3. Edo practices at the right difficulty instead of copying a direct answer.
4. Mentora updates mastery and recommends what to review.

**Aha moment:**

```text
The learner knows what to do next without being handed the answer.
```

**Visual:**

- 4-step storyboard: Stuck / Hint / Practice / Next Step.
- 1 product screenshot in the center.
- 3-4 annotation callouts only.
- Do not walk through every button.
- Do not make mentor dashboard the aha moment.

## 5. Early Usage

**Goal:** Prove that the product is not just an idea, without overclaiming traction.

**Main message:** Early learners are already asking, practicing, and reviewing.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Early learners are already asking, practicing, and reviewing.
```

**Metric cards:**

- 828 unique visitors.
- 292 active learners.
- 5,335 questions answered.
- 584 quiz completions.
- 118 peak weekly active learners.

**Insight strip:**

```text
35% of visitors became active learners; active learners answered 18+ questions on average.
```

**Source note:**

```text
Source: PostHog, Jun 2-Jul 6, 2026. Early usage signal, not PMF.
```

**Do not call this slide:**

- Traction.
- PMF.
- Proven learning outcomes.

This is early usage validation.

## 6. Market Wedge

**Goal:** Show a focused but expandable starting market.

**Main message:** Start with AI cohorts, then expand across programming, university, and workforce learning.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Start with AI cohorts; expand across programming, university, and workforce learning.
```

**Wedge funnel:**

- AI intensive cohorts.
- Programming bootcamps.
- University AI/programming courses.
- Workforce AI upskilling.

**Vietnam-first evidence context to include if sourced:**

- AI Thực Chiến target: 20,000 AI talents.
- Cohort pattern: 500 learners per cohort.
- Strong applicant demand where cited.

**Visual:**

- Use a wedge funnel or concentric circles.
- Do not spend too much slide space on TAM.
- The first wedge should be narrow but expandable.
- Mascot optional and very small.

## 7. GTM / Buyer Path

**Goal:** Explain how a student-first product can become a paid cohort deployment.

**Main message:** Pilot with one cohort, prove learner usage, then sell the rollout.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Pilot with one cohort, prove learner usage, then sell the rollout.
```

**GTM flow:**

```text
4-week pilot -> usage report -> paid cohort -> multi-cohort rollout
```

**Buyer path:**

- Primary user: AI learners.
- Initial buyer: bootcamp, program operator, university AI course, or training provider.
- Commercial reason: operators get evidence of learner engagement, citation trust, practice completion, and progress signals.

**Important framing:**

```text
The initial product experience is learner-first. Commercial expansion comes from cohort-level deployment once usage and learning signals are proven.
```

**Suggested pricing hypothesis for appendix or speaker notes:**

- Pilot 4 weeks: USD 500-1,000 per cohort, up to 100 active learners.
- Cohort Standard: USD 300-500 per cohort/month + USD 3-8 per active learner/month.
- Institution: custom by cohort count, SLA, and security review.

## 8. Competition / Moat

**Goal:** Clarify why Mentora is not just a chatbot, LMS, or quiz tool.

**Main message:** Chatbots answer. LMSs host. Mentora builds the learner’s feedback loop.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Chatbots answer. LMSs host. Mentora builds the learner’s feedback loop.
```

**Comparison groups:**

- Generic AI chatbot.
- LMS.
- Quiz tool.
- Mentora.

**Recommended matrix dimensions:**

- Official course grounding.
- Socratic hints.
- Adaptive practice.
- Mastery tracking.
- Next-review guidance.

**Moat footer:**

```text
The model is replaceable. The course-grounded learning loop is the asset.
```

**Moat explanation:**

```text
Every question, hint, quiz attempt, and mastery update improves the next review path.
```

**Visual:**

- Comparison matrix with 4-5 rows.
- Add the moat footer as a strong bottom line.
- Use checkmarks sparingly.
- Avoid claiming competitors have no capability unless verified.

## 9. Team + Ask

**Goal:** End with a specific pilot ask.

**Main message:** Mentora is looking for one AI cohort to prove usage, trust, and progress in 4 weeks.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
We are looking for one AI cohort to prove usage, trust, and progress in 4 weeks.
```

**Ask:**

- 50-100 learners.
- Official course materials.
- Student access.
- Weekly feedback loop.
- Shared success metrics.

**Proof to collect:**

- Student usage.
- Citation trust.
- Practice completion.
- Mastery/progress signals.

**Team compression:**

Keep team short unless the team is a major proof point. For each person, show:

- What they own.
- Why they are credible.
- What they shipped.

**Visual:**

- Ask card as the dominant visual.
- Small team strip or mini cards.
- Optional QR code to demo or landing page.
- Sofi and Edo can appear together, but keep the ask readable.

## Appendix

Use appendix slides for details that should not slow down the live pitch.

Recommended appendix sections:

- Learner persona detail.
- Roadmap.
- Early usage detailed report.
- Learning funnel.
- Pain validation detail: full 6-post chart, 458 engagement breakdown.
- Market sources.
- Competitor source list.
- Technical architecture.
- RAG pipeline.
- Citation validation.
- Adaptive learning algorithm.
- Elo/BKT/ZPD details.
- Academic integrity guardrails.
- Pilot measurement plan.
- Product screenshots.
- Future mentor/cohort reporting layer.

## Appendix Slide: Roadmap

**Goal:** Show maturity and future direction only if asked.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Today: learner loop. Next: pilot evidence. Later: cohort intelligence.
```

**Now: Learner loop**

- Citation quality.
- Socratic hint quality.
- Adaptive practice flow.

**Next: Pilot evidence**

- 4-week AI cohort pilot.
- Usage and completion measurement.
- Citation quality and learning signal report.

**Later: Cohort intelligence**

- Mentor weak-concept view.
- Cohort reports.
- LMS integration.

## Appendix Slide: Future Cohort Layer

**Goal:** Be transparent that mentor/operator features are future expansion, not the current polished core.

**Recommended headline:**

```text
Future layer: cohort visibility for mentors and program operators.
```

**Content:**

- Weak-concept view.
- Cohort learning reports.
- Repeated-question insights.
- LMS integration.

**Framing:**

```text
Learner-first product today; cohort intelligence layer next.
```

## Review Checklist

- Main deck has 9 slides maximum.
- Title clearly contrasts understanding vs copying.
- Problem slide combines learner persona and evidence.
- Problem slide centers learner pain, not mentor pain.
- Solution avoids algorithm jargon.
- Demo slide is a learner story with stuck, hint, practice, and next step.
- Early Usage is in the main deck but not called traction or PMF.
- GTM explains why one cohort pilot can convert into a paid rollout.
- Competition slide says the learning loop is the asset, not the model.
- Roadmap is appendix, not main deck.
- Mentor/operator layer is framed as future expansion.
- Mascots support the narrative but do not dominate data, product, or chart slides.
- Each slide has one main message.
- Main deck can be presented in 5-7 minutes.
