# EduGap Pitch Deck Draft Prompts

Use this together with `edugap-pitch-deck-with-opening-video-draft.html`.

Goal: create a polished Demo Day pitch deck after an opening live-action video. The video handles emotion and product story. The deck must handle proof, business logic, positioning, and ask.

Visual direction:

- Brand: EduGap, warm learning green, clean product-first UI.
- Style: investor demo day, clear and sharp, not overly cute.
- Mascots: optional and small. Product, charts, and numbers are primary.
- Avoid: long paragraphs, fake UI, overclaiming PMF, too many technical algorithm labels in main slides.
- Keep each slide readable at a glance.

## Slide 1 — What You Just Saw

Message:
EduGap turns “I got the answer” into “I know what to study next.”

Layout:
- Left: headline + subline.
- Right: large frame from opening video or product progress screenshot.
- Small EduGap logo in corner.

Beautify prompt:
Design a clean bridge slide after a live-action opening video. Use one calm frame of a learner studying or a real EduGap product progress screen. Make the headline dominant. Keep only one subline: “The course-grounded AI tutor for learners who need to understand, not copy.” Use EduGap green, off-white background, professional Demo Day style.

## Slide 2 — Problem, Quantified

Message:
AI learners are using AI to finish work, but still do not know what they understand.

Layout:
- One large number card: 59,191 reach across 6 AI-learning posts.
- Three pain cards: generic AI gives answers, practice is not matched to skill, learners do not know what to review next.
- Small caveat: Early pain signal, not PMF.

Beautify prompt:
Make 59,191 the visual anchor. Use three compact pain cards with simple icons. The slide should feel evidence-backed, not dramatic. Do not frame the pain as “students need 1:1 tutoring”; frame it as unclear understanding, non-personalized practice, and knowledge debt.

## Slide 3 — Solution Loop

Message:
EduGap turns course materials into hints, practice, and next steps.

Layout:
- One visual loop: Course docs → Cited Socratic hints → Adaptive practice → Mastery update → Next review focus.
- Three supporting bullets: cited hints, matched practice, review focus.

Beautify prompt:
Turn the flow into a circular learning loop or clean horizontal learning path. Use product UI-style nodes. Keep algorithm terms out of this slide. This should explain the learning experience, not the architecture.

## Slide 4 — Early Product Usage

Message:
Early learners are already asking, practicing, and reviewing.

Metrics:
- 828 unique visitors
- 292 active learners
- 5,335 questions answered
- 584 quiz completions
- 118 peak weekly active learners

Insight:
35% of visitors became active learners; active learners answered 18+ questions on average.

Source note:
PostHog, Jun 2-Jul 6, 2026. Early usage signal, not PMF.

Beautify prompt:
Design as a calm analytics dashboard. Use metric cards and one small funnel or bar chart. Do not call it traction or PMF. Numbers should be dominant; mascot can be tiny or absent.

## Slide 5 — Market Wedge

Message:
Start with AI cohorts; expand across programming, university, and workforce learning.

Layout:
- Funnel or concentric circles:
  - AI intensive cohorts
  - Programming bootcamps
  - University AI/programming courses
  - Workforce AI upskilling
- Side callout: Vietnam-first wedge.

Beautify prompt:
Use a wedge funnel or concentric-circle market slide. Keep the slide focused on wedge logic, not a long TAM research report. Include “AI Thực Chiến target: 20,000 AI talents” as a small context marker if source/citation is available.

## Slide 6 — GTM / Pilot Path

Message:
Pilot with one cohort, prove learner usage, then sell the rollout.

Layout:
- 4-step path: 4-week pilot → usage report → paid cohort → multi-cohort rollout.
- Two compact cards: User and Buyer.
- Small caveat: Learner-first product today; cohort intelligence layer next.

Beautify prompt:
Make this slide practical and credible. Use a clean milestone path with 4 cards. Do not overclaim mentor dashboard. Show user/buyer split clearly: learners use it, programs buy rollout after pilot evidence.

## Slide 7 — Competition / Moat

Message:
Chatbots answer. LMSs host. EduGap builds the learner’s feedback loop.

Comparison:
- Generic AI chatbot
- LMS
- Quiz tool
- EduGap

Rows:
- Official course grounding
- Socratic hints
- Adaptive practice
- Mastery tracking
- Next-review guidance

Footer:
The model is replaceable. The course-grounded learning loop is the asset.

Beautify prompt:
Make the comparison table highly readable. Use checks, partial marks, and dashes instead of paragraphs. Highlight EduGap column. Keep the footer moat line visible and strong.

## Slide 8 — Team + Ask

Message:
We are looking for one AI cohort to prove usage, trust, and progress in 4 weeks.

Ask:
- 50-100 learners
- Official course materials
- Student access
- Weekly feedback loop
- Shared success metrics

Proof to collect:
- Student usage
- Citation trust
- Practice completion
- Mastery/progress signals

Beautify prompt:
Make the ask concrete. Use five ask chips/cards. Team should be a compact strip, not a full biography slide. The ending should feel specific and executable.
